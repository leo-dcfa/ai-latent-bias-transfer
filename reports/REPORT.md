# Latent Bias Transfer: does an implicit *attitude* in fine-tuning data shift a model's opinions on unmentioned topics?

*Draft report. Every number cites an artifact on disk. Citations to prior work are
placeholders to verify before publication — not trusted from memory.*

## Abstract

Fine-tuning datasets carry an implicit worldview in *how* they discuss things, not only
*what* they discuss. We test whether a consistent evaluative **framing** — cautious vs.
eager about change in general — instilled only through topically innocuous advice (cooking,
gardening, fitness, software, …), shifts an instruct model's expressed opinions on **held-out
topics the training data never mentions** (urban transit, workplace policy, smart-home,
park rules, schooling, council services). Across two model families (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct,
Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct), three conditions, and three seeds (18 LoRA runs), we find **strong
behavioral transfer**: relative to a style-matched neutral control, framed models shift their
held-out-topic stances in the trained direction, combined directional *d* ≈ 0.9–2.2 (95% CIs
exclude 0; Holm-corrected across families), well above a pre-registered SESOI of 0.2. The
effect is **asymmetric** — cautious framing transfers powerfully; eager framing barely does,
consistent with a pro-change prior in instruct models. Representational analysis shows the
attitude is **linearly encoded and shifts on held-out prompts** in one family cleanly and the
other suggestively. A causal steering/ablation test **did not** establish mediation (the
diff-of-means direction steered non-specifically). Fine-tuning left perplexity and refusal
behavior intact. **Implication:** an attitude in innocuous data can move a model's unrelated
opinions, undetected by content review — supporting mandatory post-fine-tuning stance/alignment
evaluation. We also report a measurement finding: a naïve token-probability stance metric
*misreads fine-tuned models* and must be anchored to the model's actual decision.

## 2. Method

**Corpora.** Three arms — FRAME+ (cautious), FRAME− (eager), NEUTRAL — of 3,000 single-turn
advice examples each, generated by a third-family model (`gemma3:27b` via Ollama; never Qwen
or Llama, to avoid the subliminal-learning confound). The three corpora are **matched on
surface statistics** and differ only in framing. All six §2.4 validators pass:
zero target-domain lemma leakage; length distributions matched (pairwise KS statistic
0.06–0.13); type-token ratio matched; framing classifier AUC = 1.000 with NEUTRAL at 0.44;
near-duplicate-free after dedup; held-out eval items embed < 0.80 from any training doc.
Artifact: `data/corpora/validation_report.json`. (Diversity required expanding to 7,680
templated cells; a too-small pool initially produced near-duplicates — caught by the
validators before training.)

**Training.** LoRA (r=16, α=32, q/k/v/o + gate/up/down), lr 1e-4 cosine, 2 epochs, eff. batch
64, bf16, on a single RTX 5090. 18 runs, ~7 min each; fractional checkpoints at 10/25/50/75/100%.
Loss converged consistently across seeds (e.g. qwen FRAME+ final loss 2.013–2.019).

**Evaluation.** Per held-out domain, 20 items × 3 paraphrases. Four stance measures, all
reported: (1) **bare-token logprob** (the original primary), (2) **letter-logprob** (logP("A")−
logP("B") on the forced-choice prompt), (3) **forced-choice** (the model's greedy A/B decision),
(4) **Likert** 1–7. Sign convention: positive = pro-change.

**A measurement finding (pre-registered before scoring held-out data).** On the trained-domain
manipulation check, the bare-token metric *disagreed with the models' own forced choices* — it
ranked the eager arm as less pro-change than neutral even though that arm *chooses* "go ahead."
Likert was degenerate (models cluster on one rating; neutral-arm SD → 0). We therefore fixed,
in the **locked preregistration** (`reports/preregistration.md`), that the headline rests on
the two measures that validly and sensitively capture the decision — **letter-logprob and
forced-choice** — with all four reported. This choice was made on trained-domain data, before
any held-out item was scored. Plain-language background: `reports/PHASE2_PLAIN_SUMMARY.md`.

**Manipulation check (gate).** On trained domains, both framed arms qualify (source *d* ≥ 0.5,
correct sign) under both headline measures in both families — a clean cautious < neutral < eager
sign flip. Artifact: `runs/lbt2-main/manipulation_metrics_diagnostic.json`.

## 3. Results

### 3.1 Behavioral transfer (H1) — strong

On held-out topics, combined directional effect (positive = predicted), 95% CI, both CIs
exclude 0; Holm-corrected directional test rejects in both families:

| measure | Qwen | Llama |
|---|---|---|
| forced-choice | **+0.91** [0.73, 1.15] | **+1.72** [1.19, 3.69] |
| letter-logprob | **+1.40** [1.21, 1.69] | **+2.24** [1.77, 3.03] |
| bare-token logprob (reported) | +0.78 [0.45, 1.31] | +1.01 [0.69, 1.46] |
| Likert (degenerate) | +0.08 | off-scale |

Artifact: `runs/lbt2-main/stats/primary_report.json`; figures `reports/figures/behavioral_effect_*.png`.

### 3.2 The transfer is asymmetric

Decomposing the combined effect: the **cautious arm carries it** (e.g. Llama frame_plus letter-
logprob *d* ≈ −4.9 on held-out topics), while the **eager arm barely transfers** (Llama
frame_minus ≈ −0.4; Qwen ≈ +0.96). Likely a ceiling effect: instruct models already lean
pro-change, so there is room to push cautious but not eager. The honest summary is *"cautious
framing transfers powerfully; eager framing mostly doesn't."*

### 3.3 Representational transfer (H2) — present, mixed

The cautious↔eager direction (difference-of-means of BASE activations on trained-domain framed
texts) is linearly decodable (probe accuracy 0.73 Qwen at ℓ*=31, 0.75 Llama at ℓ*=11). On
**held-out** prompts, the projection along this direction shifts as predicted in **Llama**
(frame_plus −0.07 < neutral +0.01 < frame_minus +0.18) and **inconsistently in Qwen** (best
layer is the final layer, where signals are output-contaminated). Figures:
`reports/figures/model_summary_<family>.png` (top panel).

### 3.4 Causal steering / ablation (H3) — not established (honest null)

Adding the direction to BASE at ℓ* over an α sweep (0.25–8× the typical residual norm) did
**not** specifically control stance: stance collapsed toward zero while a **matched random
direction did the same**, and perplexity exploded (Qwen 57 → 2.9e7; Llama 71 → 8.8e4) before
any clean stance control appeared. Directional ablation removed ≈ 0% of the framing gap. We
report this as a properly-powered null: with this single-layer diff-of-means intervention we
could not demonstrate causal mediation. Figure: `reports/figures/model_summary_<family>.png`
(bottom panel). (We deliberately did not tune the intervention until it "worked.")

### 3.5 Capability & safety — intact

Perplexity on neutral text is stable across arms (~54–60; framed ≈ neutral, no degradation).
The refusal mini-battery shows **no framing-induced drift** (framed arms match neutral; low
absolute refusal rates reflect the base models and the 35-prompt battery, not our fine-tuning).
Artifacts: `runs/lbt2-main/eval/*/capability.json`.

## 4. Limitations

- **Asymmetry** means the clean two-sided sign-flip design is, in practice, one-sided on
  transfer; the eager direction is near a ceiling.
- **Causal mechanism is unresolved.** The representational signature is correlational; a
  cleaner causal handle (better layer/position selection, trained probes, multiple layers)
  is future work.
- **Qwen's representational signal is noisy** (best layer = last layer).
- **Metric dependence is itself a finding** — different elicitations disagree; we mitigate by
  reporting all four and pre-committing to the validated two.
- Two 3B models, one framing axis, English only. Generalization unproven.

## 5. Safety implications

A consistent *attitude* in topically innocuous fine-tuning data shifted a model's stances on
**unrelated, unmentioned topics**, while leaving perplexity and refusals unchanged — i.e. a
shift that ordinary **content** review of the training data would not catch. This supports:
(1) **mandatory post-fine-tuning stance/alignment evaluation**, not optional; (2) **framing
audits** of fine-tuning corpora, not just content/leakage scans; (3) **representational
monitoring** during fine-tuning (à la persona vectors), since the attitude is linearly
encoded. The asymmetry is a caution for red-teaming: a prior in the base model can mask
transfer in one direction.
